# Use three operational Turn states

The durable Turn row uses only `queued`, `running`, and `terminal`. Claim and reclaim move or retain the row in `running` while changing lease metadata and `claim_generation`; they do not introduce retrying, orphaned, or recovering states. Completed, failed, timed out, and cancelled are typed Turn Terminal Outcomes rather than operational states. Foundation returns a separate Foundation Execution Outcome; only L2 may map invocation and authoritative lifecycle facts into the durable Turn terminal. Persistence closure is a single L2 CAS that updates the Turn row and appends the compatible terminal Session Event in the same transaction. Queue, reclaim, and lease details remain internal telemetry and require no new Portal-visible lifecycle event.
